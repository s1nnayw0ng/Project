Swimming Spots

Explore unbelievable wild swimming spots in the united kingdom & Ireland

Forty Foot

The Forty Foot, a historic bathing pool at the southern tip of Dublin Bay, has been named among the best places to swim in the world by the NYT. Once a male only swimming spot, The Forty Foot near Dún Laoghaire in South Dublin is now open to everyone young and old to swim there. People have been swimming in the Irish Sea all year round for some 250 years.

Gormire Lake in Hambleton, North Yorkshire

Gormire Lake - also known as the White Mere - was formed by glacial erosion over 20,000 years ago, making it a remnant of the last Ice Age, its one of only four natural lakes in Yorkshire. Gormire roughly translates as 'filthy swamp', but that has not stopped the lake becoming a go-to choice for plenty of wild swimmers. The lake in North York Moors National Park has no inflow or major outflow of water and is thought to be fed by an underground spring.

Hampstead Heath Swimming Ponds

The Hampstead Ponds consist of three ponds on the Heath's southwest corner, towards South End Green. Hampstead is near to Hampstead Heath overground station. Hampstead ponds no. 1 is strictly a nature/wildlife pond, Hampstead pond no. 2 permits fishing. Hampstead Pond no. 3 is the mixed bathing pond, where both sexes may swim. Only lifeguarded and only open to the public outside of the winter season. It's open to the Public from 7am - 8:30pm, and booking is required for most slots.

Llyn Cau

Llyn Cau, It lies at the southern end of the Snowdonia National Park near the town of Dolgellau. The peak, which is one of the most popular in Wales for walkers and hikers, This is a large glacial lake nestled between the walls of the mountain. There is a quite a difficult walk to the lake with 350 metres of ascent. Appropriate footwear and clothing should be worn.

Loch Ness

Dive into the largest body of freshwater in the UK and brave the chilly temperatures of Loch Ness for one of the UK’s most iconic open water swims. Loch Nessis a large, deep, freshwater loch in the Scottish Highlands extending for approximately 37 kilometres (23 miles) southwest of Inverness. Its surface is 16 metres (52 feet) above sea level. Loch Ness is best known for alleged sightings of the "Loch Ness Monster".

Port Meadow at River Thames,Oxford, UK

Port Meadow is one of the largest open spaces in the north of city (136.9ha). With the River Thames flowing through the heart of the meadow the flood plains are home to many species of cattle, horses and wildfowl. Swim in the river Thames without all the city grub - the water in Port Meadow is so clear it's hard to believe that it is the same river as the murky trail that snakes throught London. The grassy banks have natural bays, ideal for sitting down with a picnic punctuated by repeated dips.

Respryn Bridge

Respryn Bridge - Found in the woodland of the National Trust's Lanhydrock estate in Bodmin, this is easily accessible by a footpath just a short walk away from the car park. With a small beach near the footbridge, this swim is uniquely beautiful in the spring when the forest floor is covered in a carpet of bluebells.

Thrupenny Pool Bundoran

Bundoran boasts two outdoor sea water swimming pools along its coastline – the Thrupenny Pool and the West End Pool (Nun’s Pool). With the increasing popularity of sea and open water swimming, these pools provide safe, enclosed swimming area where the lengths can be racked up.
